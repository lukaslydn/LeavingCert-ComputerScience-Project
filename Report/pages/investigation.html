Investigation

  • I was interested in how software models can be used to make meaningful environmental predictions by applying my skills from leaving cert computer science.
  • The winter of 2024/2025 saw significant damage from Storm Darragh and Storm Éowyn, impacting over 26,000 hectares of Irish forest, demonstrating how extreme weather events can cause widespread damage to forests, with strong winds leading to fallen trees, and long-term ecological stress.
  • Highlighting the urgent need for the predictive modelling my project will aim to provide.

Existing solution

  • COFORD Windthrow Model primary reference for windblow prediction in Ireland.
    • Coford publication
  • It calculates risk based on altitude, location, soil type, and management (thinning), aiding in forestry, planning, and scheduling
  • Developed by:
    • UCD
    • Teagasc
    • Coillte

Model / Alogrithm

  • I will choose this model as it is suitable because it handles future weather predictions effectively.
  • I will choose this approach as I have worked with it before and future weather is unpredictable, which the monte carlo accounts for

Formulas and Risk setpoints

  • To move beyond simple rule-based thresholds (e.g., "wind speed > 80km/h"), My project will use a weighted multi-variable risk index
  • Outputting probabilistic variance: utilizing Monte Carlo simulations to account for the uncertainty

Potential end users

  • Coillte CEO Imelda Hurney
    • "Our vision is to create a sustainable future for all by delivering the multiple benefits of forestry and leaving an enriched resource for the next generation."
  • Emergency services
    • The HSE reported that hospital capacity was heavily impacted by delayed discharges and the need to relocate GPs and services into HSE premises to maintain "safe services"
  • Energy companies
    • At the peak of the storm, 768,000 customers lost power
    • The storm cost ESB approximately €100 million in network repairs across the Republic and Northern Ireland.

Relevance to forests

  • Due to climate change extreme storms which damage forestry are becoming increasing prevalent.
  • With prior notification, teams can perform targeted emergency pruning or thinning to minimise forestry loss

Embedded system selection

  • Mirco:bit : While a Raspberry Pi offers more processing power, the micro:bit is significantly more cost-effective and accessible for a localized forest deployment
  • I will use a cup anemometer because wind speed is the most heavily weighted variable in forest risk indices
  • By collecting real-world wind and temp data, I can feed these values into my Python model to satisfy the advanced requirement of using embedded data for predictive analysis
